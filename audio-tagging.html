Multi-label audio tagging on 4× V100

Domain   multi-label audio classification Hardware   4× V100-SXM2-32GB Result   AudioSet mAP at parity with PANNs (0.431)

The system is a from-scratch CNN-based multi-label audio tagger trained on AudioSet-unbalanced and jointly finetuned on a unified label space spanning AudioSet, FSD50K, VGGSound, VocalSound, and Nonspeech7k. It runs on a 4× V100-SXM2-32GB node and reaches AudioSet eval mAP at parity with the published PANNs CNN14 reference (0.431), with strong per-class AP on a target set of human-vocal events including cough, sneeze, laughter, baby cry, snoring, footsteps, breathing, and clapping. The training pipeline runs end to end in 5 to 7 wall-clock days, versus the 5 to 6 weeks an SSL alternative such as BEATs would consume on the same hardware.

01Scope

The target capability is robust multi-label tagging across an open vocabulary of acoustic events, with particular emphasis on human-vocal sounds that downstream consumers care about most. The hardware budget (4× V100-SXM2-32GB) is half what the original PANNs CNN14 release used and roughly an order of magnitude below what current SSL-frontier work assumes. The design therefore commits firmly to from-scratch supervised training rather than chasing the SSL leaderboard, and recovers competitive numbers through careful loss, sampling, and label-space engineering.

The system builds on prior in-house work, namely a from-scratch ResNet-18 baseline for the ESC-50 benchmark that reaches roughly 83% mean accuracy across the official 5-fold split. The log-mel frontend (32 kHz, 128 mel bins, hop 320), energy-preserving waveform mixup, EMA with batch-norm refresh, and the careful fp32 wrapping of the spectrogram path all carry over directly into the larger system.

02Datasets and label space

AudioSet, FSD50K, and VGGSound are the three corpora that anchor any serious 2026 audio tagger. The system uses all three plus VocalSound and Nonspeech7k, with the careful observation that VGGSound is single-positive-label: most clips contain unannotated positives for other classes. Treating those unlabeled classes as confirmed negatives systematically degrades per-class precision; the system instead carries a don't-care mask on VGGSound rows, masking unlabeled classes out of the loss entirely.

The three taxonomies do not naively share a common space. AudioSet's 632-node hierarchical ontology (527 used) is the natural superset; FSD50K's 200 classes map cleanly onto AudioSet MIDs; VGGSound's flat 309-class taxonomy requires a manually curated mapping that splits into four buckets:

  • Direct leaf match (~150 classes): "dog barking" maps to AudioSet Bark.
  • Coarsened leaf (~70): VGGSound's verb-ful classes such as "playing electric guitar" map to AudioSet's instrument leaf Electric guitar after dropping the action.
  • Intermediate-only (~50): VGGSound's "baby laughing" folds into AudioSet's Laughter ancestor when no fine-grained leaf exists.
  • No equivalent (~30): fine-grained animals (sea lion, orca) absent from AudioSet are added as additional columns or folded into Animal.

The mapping is anchored on the SALT taxonomy (Stamatiadis et al. 2024), which aligns 24+ tagging datasets under one schema, and combined with Hierarchical Label Propagation (Tuncay et al., ICASSP 2025): every positive leaf is propagated up the AudioSet DAG, lifting the mean labels-per-clip from 1.98 to 2.39 and consistently improving mAP on multiple architectures. HLP is essential when mixing leaf-only VGGSound with multi-level AudioSet without it, the model receives systematically inconsistent supervision.

License posture is conservative: FSD50K is unambiguously redistributable under CC-BY; AudioSet is consumed by ID list with audio scraped or sourced from public dataset mirrors and validated against the original CSV manifests; VGGSound audio is scraped from YouTube against the released CSV; VocalSound and Nonspeech7k are obtained from their respective release pages.

03Architecture choice

Three families of model were evaluated against the constraint of no ImageNet weights, no SSL pretraining, and no distillation from a pretrained teacher. The from-scratch supervised column is the only one that matters for this hardware budget:

Model Params From-scratch AS mAP Verdict
PANNs CNN14 80.7 M 0.431 Selected. The recipe.
Wavegram-Logmel-CNN ~81 M 0.439 Backup; +0.008 mAP at +10% compute.
ConvNeXt-AT ~28 M ~0.448 Best modern-CNN result. Optional upgrade at smaller param count.
AST 87 M ~0.366 Catastrophic without ImageNet. Disqualified.
HTS-AT, PaSST-S 31 to 86 M ~0.42 to 0.44 No win over CNN14 from scratch; higher per-step cost.
BEATs / EAT / Dasheng / CED 86 to 1200 M 0.486 to 0.506 with SSL/distill Unreachable on 4× V100 in any reasonable time budget.

Every transformer architecture loses 0.04 to 0.08 mAP in the from-scratch regime relative to its ImageNet-pretrained number. Every SSL recipe assumes either 16+ V100 for weeks or a teacher ensemble that itself depends on ImageNet. CNN14 is the rational choice; the optional upgrade path is ConvNeXt-AT for the same-or-better mAP at a third the parameters.

04Training and loss design

Three engineering decisions account for most of the gap between a naive baseline and competitive numbers:

  1. Class-uniform sampling. AudioSet's head-to-tail ratio is roughly 15,000 to 1: Music and Speech each dominate ~40% of clips while tail classes appear in tens. The PANNs sampler picks a class uniformly, then a clip from that class uniformly; the clip retains its full multi-hot target. This alone produces a ~3 mAP gap over random clip sampling on AudioSet.
  2. Asymmetric Loss (Ridnik et al. 2021) replaces vanilla BCE. AudioSet has a 99.6% per-clip negative rate; ASL applies different focal exponents to positives and negatives and probability-shifts easy negatives below a small margin to zero gradient, which is exactly the right inductive bias for this imbalance regime. ASL outperforms class-balanced focal, pos-weighted BCE, and plain BCE by 1 to 3 mAP across multiple DCASE entries.
  3. Convex multi-hot mixup. Energy-preserving waveform mixup is paired with a convex combination of multi-hot targets (matching PANNs and PaSST), not with the set-union variant. The convex formulation correctly reflects that a mixed clip contains each event at fractional intensity, not at full intensity.

SpecAugment runs on the spectrogram with independent (per-sample) frequency and time masks, not the deceptively-named "iid" default that uses the same mask coordinates across the whole batch. EMA of model weights is maintained for evaluation, with a batch-norm refresh pass before each evaluation so the EMA model's running statistics match its parameters. The mel frontend executes in fp32 inside an autocast-disabled scope while the convolutional trunk runs in fp16, because fp16 squaring of small magnitudes underflows and corrupts the subsequent log step.

05Why not SSL pretraining

Self-supervised audio pretraining is the headline frontier in 2025 to 2026, with SSLAM, BEATs, EAT, CED, and Dasheng all reporting AudioSet mAP at or above 0.486. None of these are reachable on a 4× V100 budget in a useful timeframe.

The published BEATs procedure used 16× V100-SXM2-32GB for ~75 hours per iteration, with three iterations and additional tokenizer-training rounds, totaling roughly 3,600 V100-hours. On 4× V100 that is ~12.5 wall-clock days per iteration and 5 to 6 weeks for the full procedure. Audio-MAE used 64× V100 for 36 hours, mapping to ~24 wall-clock days on 4× V100. EAT is the cheapest credible SSL recipe and still takes longer than supervised CNN14 while requiring careful replication of the data2vec 2.0 inverse-block-mask machinery to converge to its quoted number.

Decision

The supervised CNN14 pipeline reaches 0.431 mAP in 5 to 7 days. The features it learns transfer cleanly to ESC-50, FSD50K, VGGSound, and downstream finetuning. There is no SSL recipe that delivers a usable win inside this hardware envelope.

06Human-vocal events

The target class set (laughter, cough, sneeze, baby cry, snoring, footsteps, clapping, breathing, throat clearing, sniff, yawn) lives almost entirely in the AudioSet Human sounds subtree under five children: human voice, respiratory sounds, human locomotion, hand sounds, and human group actions. Yawn is not a leaf class anywhere in AudioSet; the closest siblings are sigh, pant, and gasp.

AudioSet alone is starved for several of these classes. Throat clearing, for example, has 355 clips in AudioSet versus 3,504 in VocalSound (Gong, Yu, Glass, ICASSP 2022): 21,024 clips from 3,365 unique speakers covering laughter, sigh, cough, throat clearing, sneeze, and sniff at roughly 3,500 clips per class. The published paper reports a relative +41.9% improvement in vocal-class precision when VocalSound is added to FSD50K training. The system co-trains on VocalSound with AudioSet and FSD50K rather than training on it alone, since VocalSound clips are acted on Mechanical Turk and the in-the-wild distribution is preserved by the larger corpora.

Nonspeech7k (Rashid et al., 2023) closes the yawn gap with a 7-class corpus (breathing, cough, cry, laugh, scream, sneeze, yawn) of roughly 6,300 clips. The Donate-a-Cry corpus contributes ~1,128 dedicated baby-cry clips beyond AudioSet's noisier class. ESC-50's 10-class "Human, non-speech sounds" group is held out as a class-specific sanity check rather than included in training, since 40 clips per class is too small to influence a 2 M-clip run but is well-suited to per-class diagnostics.

07Operational characteristics

The full pipeline runs in two stages. Stage 1 is supervised pretraining on AudioSet-unbalanced for 600 k iterations at effective batch 256 (64 per GPU on 4 GPUs) with AdamW, cosine learning-rate schedule, and a 10-epoch warmup. Stage 2 is joint finetuning on the unified label space combining AudioSet-balanced, FSD50K-dev, VGGSound-train, VocalSound, and Nonspeech7k for 30 to 50 epochs at a reduced learning rate.

NCCL all-reduce on ~80 M fp16 parameters fully overlaps with the backward pass on NVLink- SXM2 V100; observed scaling efficiency is 95 to 97% and the bottleneck is data IO rather than compute. Mel features are precomputed offline and served from HDF5 shards on Gen4 NVMe. At sustained 8 GB/s of mel data per second, a single PCIe Gen3 NVMe is insufficient and a SATA SSD will collapse GPU utilisation to 30 to 40%; the production setup uses Gen4 NVMe in RAID-0 or rides the Linux page cache on a high-RAM host after the first epoch warms it.

End-to-end Stage-1 wall clock is 5 to 7 days, including ~10 to 15% data-loader overhead. Stage 2 adds roughly one further day. Evaluation reports per-class AP on AudioSet eval, FSD50K eval, VocalSound eval, and the ESC-50 Human-sounds 10-class hold-out as the standard monitoring suite, alongside the headline mAP and AUC numbers.

Summary

A from-scratch supervised CNN, trained with PANNs-style class-uniform sampling and Asymmetric Loss on AudioSet-unbalanced and jointly finetuned on a SALT-unified label space with VocalSound and Nonspeech7k, reaches AudioSet eval mAP at parity with the published 0.431 reference and delivers strong per-class AP on the human-vocal target set, in 5 to 7 wall-clock days on 4× V100. The non-obvious wins are SALT plus Hierarchical Label Propagation for clean cross-dataset label fusion, ASL over plain BCE for the extreme negative imbalance, the don't-care mask for VGGSound's single-positive-label semantics, and VocalSound as a per-class data-volume booster on six of the eleven target events.
